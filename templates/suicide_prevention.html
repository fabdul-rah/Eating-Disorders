About Suicide Prevention

Suicide prevention involves collective efforts to reduce the risk of suicide through awareness, education, and support. It aims to provide resources and assistance to individuals struggling with suicidal thoughts.


Causes of Suicide

Suicide can result from various complex factors, including:

  • Mental health disorders, such as depression and anxiety.
  • Relationship problems or social isolation.
  • Substance abuse and addiction.
  • Academic or work-related stress and pressure.

Warning Signs

Recognizing the warning signs of suicide is crucial. These may include:

  • Expressing thoughts of hopelessness or feeling trapped.
  • Drastic mood swings or extreme emotional distress.
  • Withdrawing from friends, family, and activities.
  • Increased use of alcohol or drugs.

How to Help

If you or someone you know is struggling, here's how you can help:

  • Call a helpline or emergency services immediately.
  • Encourage open communication and express your concern.
  • Seek professional help from mental health professionals.
  • Provide emotional support and reassurance.
